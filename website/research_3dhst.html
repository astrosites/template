UVJ Quiescent Galaxies with Hα emission

Figure 1:

The location of our mass and redshift selected galaxies in the UVJ diagram. Quiescent systems are shown as red points and star-forming systems are shown as blue points. Objects with Hα detections are identified with purple crosses. The box outlined by the grey dashed line identifies the region in color-color space used to fit for the UVJ dividing line: for each 3D-HST field, and for each redshift bin (0.7≤z≤1 or 1<z≤1.5), the minimum of the (U-V) - 0.88×(V-J) distribution is identified as the UVJ separation line. The dividing lines for the low-z selections are shown as grey lines and the dividing lines for the high-z selections are shown as green lines. Although most Hα detections correspond to star-forming system, 49.7 percent of quiescent objects are detected in Hα at >1σ.

Figure 2:

The location of our sample in the star formation rate-stellar mass plane. Colored points plot our sample of quiescent galaxies with Hα emission from the 3D-HST catalogs, with circular points corresponding to objects with spatially resolved Hα emission and triangular points corresponding to objects with spatially unresolved Hα emission. The color of each point corresponds to the UV+IR star formation rate of the object. Arrows indicate upper limits of the Hα-based SFRs, again with the color indicating the UV+IR SFR. Black contours identify the main sequence of UVJ star-forming systems within our sample, and the magenta solid and dashed lines show the z=1 main sequence from Whitaker et al. 2014 and the 1σ scatter respectively. Black dashed, and dot-dashed lines identify the 3σ completeness limits of 3D-HST at z=0.7 and z=1.5 (assuming AHα=0). The population of quiescent objects is predominantly made up of systems lying below the main sequence; however some objects, particularly with low masses, lie on or above the main sequence.

Figure 3:

The fraction of UVJ quiescent objects on or above the main sequence (SFR≥SFRMain Sequence-1σ) as a function of stellar mass. Here, our sample is split into two redshift bins 0.7≤z≤1.0 and 1.0<z≤1.5; blue lines correspond to objects in the lower redshift bin and green lines correspond to objects in the higher redshift bin. Shaded regions correspond to 1σ confidence intervals. Solid lines with solid shaded regions are determined from Hα-based SFRs, whereas dashed lines and hatched regions are determined using UV+IR SFR. As in Figure 2, we are likely missing some low-level, extincted star formation from low mass objects, meaning points below 109.7M⊙ (109.2M⊙) are lower limits for z>1 (z≤) sample.

Figure 4:

A: The mean position of quiescent galaxies in the UVJ diagram for both systems with Hα (as purple circles) and without Hα (as red circles) across different bins of stellar mass. The size of the circle corresponds to the number of galaxies in the bin, and the median stellar mass, as well as the fraction of objects with Hα in the stellar mass bin are listed adjacent to each set of circles. Arrows in the bottom right indicate the direction of the psudocolors we use to describe the position of objects in the UVJ diagram, both parallel and perpendicular to the UVJ dividing line ((U-V)+1.14×(V-J) and (U-V)-0.88×(V-J) respectively). Within nearly all stellar mass bins, the location of quiescent objects with Hα is identical to those without Hα; however, low mass galaxies are more likely to host Hα emission. This point is further illustrated in plot B. B: The distribution of perpendicular psudocolors for quiescent objects with (as the solid purple histogram) and without (as the solid red histogram) Hα emission. Also shown as the dashed histogram, the distribution of perpendicular psudocolors for objects with spatially resolved Hα emission. Bold lines with lightly shaded sides delineate the mean and standard deviation on the mean of the perpendicular psudocolors for the distributions. All three distributions are identical (a two-sided KS test yields a p value of 0.12), suggesting that the dividing line we use is effective at identifying objects with colors consistent with an evolved stellar population regardless of the presence of Hα emission.

Figure 5:

A: The distribution of the parallel psudocolors of quiescent objects with Hα emission (as the solid purple histogram) and those without Hα emission (as the solid red histogram). Again, the distribution of objects with spatially resolved Hα emission is shown as a dashed purple histogram. As opposed to the distribution of perpendicular psudocolors, the distribution of parallel psudocolors is significantly different for objects with and without Hα emission. In particular, objects with Hα tend to be bluer than objects without Hα, suggesting that galaxies with Hα emission tend to host a younger stellar population than those without Hα emission. B: A veronoi diagram illustrating the frequency of Hα emission for regions in stellar mass-parallel psudocolor space, with lighter colors corresponding to regions with a higher occurrence rate of Hα emission (defined as Nwith Hα/Ntotal). Black contours correspond the distribution of all quiescent objects in this space, illustrating the strong trend between stellar mass and parallel psudocolor for quiescent objects and the blue and blue and green lines indicate the mean parallel psudocolor at a given mass for objects without Hα, and objects without Hα emission respectively. The position of galaxies on this sequence is largely unaffected by the presence of Hα emission, indicating that the reddening of quiescent galaxies at higher stellar mass occurs in tandem with the decreased frequency of Hα emission.

Figure 6:

Structural properties of quiescent galaxies in the 3D-HST survey. Shown in the top panel is the distribution of the stellar density within the central 1 kpc (Σ1). The distribution of quiescent objects with Hα emission is discrepant with the distribution of objects with no detected Hα emission (a KS test finds a p value of 6×10-5). The bottom panel illustrates the location of quiescent and star-forming systems in the Σ1-n plane. Blue and red contours show the location of UVJ star-forming and quiescent objects respectively and points correspond to objects with spatially resolved Hα emission (circles) and unresolved Hα emission (triangles). Color coding corresponds to the position of the object along the red sequence as measured by (U-V)+1.14(V-J), with lighter colors further along on the red sequence. Quiescent objects with Hα appear to be transitioning from a disk-dominated structure to a buldge-dominated structure as they move along the red sequence before they become completely quenched and elliptical.

Figure 7:

The dust content of UVJ selected systems, as probed by their infrared excess (IRX=LIR/L1600). All objects shown have a secure 24 μm detection. Although the dust content of high mass objects is consistent between quiescent and star-forming galaxies, below ~1010.25, quiescent objects have higher levels of dust extinction than typical star-forming galaxies. This suggests that some low-mass objects have moved into this space due to very blue intrinsic colors and high levels of extinction.

Figure 8:

Dust-corrected U-V and V-J colors for the quiescent object in the 3D-HST sample. Extinction is either calculated from the infrared excess (for objects with 24μm detections), or the best-fitting SED (for objects with no 24μm detection). Large points correspond to the median position of objects in this space for different mass bins, with the size of the point correlated with the mass of the bin. The median position of the entire sample is shown as the large red points, the median position of the quiescent objects with Hα emission is shown as the purple points. Blue and yellow points correspond to the subsamples with 24μm detections and both 24μm and Hα detections respectively. There appears to be population of low-mass objects with blue dust-corrected colors that is extincted into UVJ quiescent space.
